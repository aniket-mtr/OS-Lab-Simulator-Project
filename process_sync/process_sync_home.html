Process synchronisation

Overview

Process Synchronization is the coordination of execution of multiple processes in a multi-process system to ensure that they access shared resources in a controlled and predictable manner. It aims to resolve the problem of race conditions and other synchronization issues in a concurrent system. The main objective of process synchronization is to ensure that multiple processes access shared resources without interfering with each other and to prevent the possibility of inconsistent data due to concurrent access. To achieve this, various synchronization techniques such as semaphores, monitors, and critical sections are used.We will look at three classical problems for process synchronisation.
They are:
1.Producer consumer problem
2.Reader Writer problem

Producer consumer problem

The Producer-Consumer problem involves coordinating producers and consumers in concurrent systems. Producers generate items while consumers consume them, requiring synchronization to prevent conflicts. Semaphores, mutexes, or condition variables are used for mutual exclusion and synchronization. Optimal solutions are crucial for robust and efficient concurrent system design.

To solve the Producer-Consumer problem:

1.Initialize two semaphores: empty_slots and filled_slots, and a mutex for mutual exclusion.
2.Producers acquire empty_slots before producing, decrement it, then acquire the mutex to access the buffer.
3.After producing, they release the mutex and signal filled_slots.
4.Consumers acquire filled_slots before consuming, decrement it, then acquire the mutex to access the buffer.
5.After consuming, they release the mutex and signal empty_slots.
6.This ensures proper synchronization, preventing buffer overflow or underflow, and ensuring mutual exclusion.

Reader Writer problem

The Reader-Writer problem in OS entails managing shared data access among multiple processes. Readers can access data simultaneously but exclusively from writers, necessitating synchronization to prevent race conditions. Solutions involve ensuring fairness, preventing writer starvation, and maintaining system performance. Semaphores, mutexes, or monitors are used for synchronization, guaranteeing data integrity and preventing deadlock.

To solve the Reader-Writer problem:

1.Initialize two semaphores: mutex for mutual exclusion and rw_mutex for reader-writer synchronization.
2.Initialize an integer variable read_count to track the number of readers.
3.Writers acquire rw_mutex, perform writing, and release it.
4.Readers increment read_count, block writers if they are the first reader, and release rw_mutex after reading.
5.After reading, readers decrement read_count and release rw_mutex if no readers are left.
6.This method ensures mutual exclusion between writers and controls access for readers, facilitating concurrent access to shared data.
